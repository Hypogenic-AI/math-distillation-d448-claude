# Downloaded Papers

1. [The Equational Theories Project: Advancing Collaborative Mathematical Research at Scale](2512.07087_equational_theories_project.pdf)
   - Authors: M. Bolan, J. Breitner, J. Brox, N. Carlini, M. Carneiro, F. van Doorn, ... T. Tao, et al. (34 authors)
   - Year: 2025
   - arXiv: 2512.07087
   - Why relevant: The foundational paper for the Mathematics Distillation Challenge. Reports on the complete determination of all 22M implications between 4694 equational laws on magmas. Documents all proof techniques (finite counterexamples, syntactic rewrites, ATPs, matching invariants, greedy constructions) that a cheat sheet must distill.

2. [The Latent Space of Equational Theories](2601.20759_latent_space_equational_theories.pdf)
   - Authors: Luis Berlioz, Paul-Andre Mellies
   - Year: 2026
   - arXiv: 2601.20759
   - Why relevant: Constructs a statistical embedding of equational theories using Stone pairings (satisfaction probabilities on random finite magmas). Shows that implications flow in a well-structured way in this latent space. Directly relevant to understanding what features make implications predictable, which informs cheat sheet design.
